Circuit de Monaco

With the many elevations shifts and narrow corners, it is perhaps the most demanding track of Formula 1 Racing. Although its circuit has changed many times during its history, it remains one of the most technically challenging circuits to be driven that all drivers love. The race circuit has many elevation changes and sharp corners, making it one of the most demanding courses in Formula 1 racing. Since to date, two drivers have crashed and ended up in the Harbour. The most famous being Alberto Ascari in 1955. It was late in the race when he crashed into the Harbour. He approached the corner too quickly and took his D50 through the barriers into the sea. Three seconds passed before his helmet appeared on the surface. Fortunately, he left the crash with only a broken nose. If the Circuit de Monaco were considered today as a race track, it would not be permitted due to its many hazards. Most notably, the course includes a tunnel. The contrast between dark and light creates a challenge for the drivers as they must react as they exit the tunnel at the fastest point of the track.
